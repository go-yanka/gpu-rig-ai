## 1. Problem statement

We operate a production FastAPI RAG system over 108,802 chunks of Indian indirect-tax law — GST (CGST/IGST/UTGST/Rules), Customs (Act/Tariff/Valuation Rules/Notifications/Circulars), Central Excise, Service Tax. Users are tax practitioners asking fact-specific questions ("place of supply for bill-to-ship-to", "ITC on motor car", "SVB procedure"). Current end-to-end trustworthiness is unacceptable: **recall@5 = 20.0%, recall@1 = 8.2%** against a 170-item human-curated gold set. Target: **recall@5 = 60-70%, end-to-end trust ≥ 95%** (answer cites the correct section/rule/notification with no hallucinated citation). Without that, the system is worse than a Google search for a CA.

## 3. Diagnosis — retrieval AND ingestion, not pure retrieval

Failure-mode taxonomy over 149 misses (`failure_modes_20260422.md`):

| Failure mode | % of misses |
|---|---:|
| Right_act_wrong_section (correct Act retrieved, wrong section) | **59.1%** |
| Notation_mismatch (expected `10(1)(b) IGST`, chunk has `10 IGST` or `section 10`) | **28.9%** |
| Wrong_domain (asked GST, got Customs/Cess) | 6.7% |
| Procedural_vs_substantive confusion | 3.4% |
| Rate_notification_miss | 2.0% |

The 88 "Right_act_wrong_section" and 43 "Notation_mismatch" misses together = **88%** of failures. They are **not** reasoning failures — they are embedding-similarity failures where the query "place of supply for services to registered person" surfaces `Section 2 IGST` (definitions) instead of `Section 12(2) IGST`.

**Ingestion red flags** (`chunk_audit_20260422.md`, 108,802 points):

- **83.17% of chunks have NO `section_ref` metadata.** Any section-aware retrieval/filtering is crippled at the source.
- **19.34% of chunks are >2,400 chars** — beyond BGE-M3's useful window; embedding gets diluted.
- **Boilerplate contamination:** the GSTIN table header `| 1. | GSTIN |…` appears **273×**, drawback tariff header **188×**, rate-table header **170×**. These near-identical chunks pollute nearest-neighbour space for every "registration / GSTIN / rate" query.
- **Parent-act inconsistency:** `GST` (34,065), `CUSTOMS` (14,120), `Customs Act 1962` (18,884) and `Customs Tariff Act 1975` (13,955) are separate parent_act labels for what users treat as one concept family. Filtering by parent_act is lossy.

**Conclusion:** even a perfect embedder can't reliably rank `12(2) IGST` above `2(11)(ii) IGST` when (a) neither chunk has structured section_ref, (b) 273 copies of a GSTIN boilerplate drown out the signal, and (c) the act label space is fragmented. Fine-tuning alone is betting that the model learns to ignore all of this. That's a bet, not a plan.

## 4. Plan

a. **Fine-tune BGE-M3** on curated (question, positive_chunk) pairs using `sentence-transformers` `MultipleNegativesRankingLoss` (in-batch negatives) + explicit hard negatives per question. Base = `BAAI/bge-m3`, 1024-dim dense head only (drop colbert/sparse heads for now).

b. **Training data** (~5,000 pairs after filtering):
   - 1,891 pairs generated by Gemini 2.5 Pro over curated CBIC source docs
   - ~300 Sonnet-generated pairs targeting HIGH-complexity scenario questions (place-of-supply, bill-to-ship-to, ITC edge cases, SVB, anti-profiteering)
   - 76 pairs hand-authored with Claude Opus for adversarial / notation-mismatch cases
   - QA filter: each pair scored on `answerable` (chunk actually answers), `realistic` (a CA would ask this), `specific` (not generic "what is GST"). Drop anything below threshold. `<TBD — QA scores pending>`
   - Diversity balance: cap per-category to avoid GST swamping (target mix ~ 50 GST / 25 Customs / 10 Service Tax / 10 Excise / 5 Others).

c. **Hard negatives**: for each question, retrieve top-20 from current Qdrant index (baseline BGE-M3), remove the gold chunk(s) and any chunk whose `section_ref` matches gold. Keep 5-7 hardest. This gives same-act-wrong-section negatives — exactly the 59% failure mode we need to break.

d. **Training setup:**
   - Runpod A100 40GB, ~1 hour wall-clock expected
   - `sentence-transformers`, batch 32 (eff. 128 with in-batch negatives), 3 epochs, lr 2e-5, warmup 10%
   - Per-epoch `InformationRetrievalEvaluator` on held-out 500 pair slice
   - Save best-by-NDCG@10 checkpoint

e. **Evaluation gate:** re-embed corpus with fine-tuned model → rebuild Qdrant collection → re-run 170-item recall audit. **Do not ship unless recall@5 ≥ 45%** (2.25× baseline). Any further work (reranker, HyDE, metadata filtering) is gated on measured lift from this step.

f. **Open sequencing question:** we have two paths and they are expensive in different ways.
   - Path A (embedder-first): 1 day to fine-tune, 2 hours to re-embed. If it lifts recall@5 to 45%+ we ship it, then fix ingestion in parallel.
   - Path B (ingestion-first): 4-8 weeks to re-chunk 108k points with (i) section-aware splitter populating `section_ref`, (ii) boilerplate deduping, (iii) unified parent_act taxonomy. Then fine-tune on cleaner data.
   We are currently leaning Path A. **Is that wrong given 83% missing section_ref?**

## 6. Open questions for the reviewer (prioritized)

1. **Sequencing (P0):** Given 83% missing `section_ref` and 273× boilerplate duplication — is fine-tuning first ("Path A") the right call, or are we polishing a broken index? If you've shipped a legal/tax RAG, which path won you more recall per engineer-week?

2. **Data scale (P0):** Is ~5K curated pairs enough for BGE-M3 full fine-tune on a shifted domain (Indian tax statute notation)? Papers we've read suggest 10K+ for dense retrievers, but BGE-M3 is already multilingual+legal-ish. Would you expect meaningful lift at 5K, or recommend we pause and generate to 15K before training?

3. **Base model (P1):** Should we drop BGE-M3 entirely for this domain and move to Qwen3-Embedding-8B, GTE-Qwen2-7B, E5-mistral-7b-instruct, or Jina-v3? BGE-M3 MTEB numbers are dated and Indian tax notation (`10(1)(b) IGST`, `Rule 3 CVR 2007`) is adversarial to subword tokenizers. A concrete recommendation with a reason would be gold.

4. **Loss function (P1):** `MultipleNegativesRankingLoss` (in-batch) vs explicit `InfoNCE` with the mined hard negatives as the actual negatives vs `CachedMultipleNegativesRankingLoss` for larger effective batches. With 5-7 hard negatives per query we suspect MNR wastes them. What's your call?

5. **Expected ceiling (P1):** On a 108K domain corpus with 5K fine-tuning pairs and hard negatives, what recall@5 lift do you expect? Absolute numbers from published or shipped systems (LegalBench-RAG, CLERC, ContractNLI, in-house tax bots) would help us sanity-check whether 45% is an ambitious floor or a sandbag target.

6. **Reranker (P2):** Should a cross-encoder reranker (bge-reranker-v2-m3, Cohere rerank-3, Jina-reranker-v2) be Phase 1 instead of or alongside fine-tuning? It's cheaper to deploy, and the "right-act-wrong-section" failure mode is textbook reranker territory.

7. **Ingestion hard-fix scope (P2):** If Path B is truly needed, what's the minimum viable re-ingest — just populate `section_ref` via regex post-hoc on existing chunks (1 day), or full re-chunk with structure-aware splitter (weeks)? Is there a published section-aware legal chunker we should adopt rather than build?
